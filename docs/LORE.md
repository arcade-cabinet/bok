# Lore — Bok: The Builder's Tome

## The Tome

The Builder's Tome — "Bok" in the old tongue — is an ancient book of impossible architecture. It holds the blueprints of worlds that never were, and the memories of builders long forgotten. Each island holds a piece of its wisdom, guarded by a creature who has grown to embody that land.

The player is a Builder: someone who can hear the Tome calling. They must sail to each island, face what guards it, and earn the right to add a new page.

## The Hub Island

A warm tropical island — lush, permanent, yours. The Hub survives death. It is home.

Four NPCs live on the Hub:

- **The Blacksmith** — A weathered figure who upgrades the Armory and Forge. Says little, means everything.
- **The Scholar** — Keeper of the Library. Studies the Tome pages you bring back. Offers ability upgrades in exchange for rare materials.
- **The Shopkeeper** — Runs the Market. Buys and sells gear. Cheerful, practical, occasionally wise.
- **The Navigator** — Stands at the central dock. Knows every island, every route, every current. The Guide.

## The Islands

Each island is infinite, procedurally generated, and distinct. They are not just terrain — they have character.

| Island | Personality |
|--------|------------|
| Whispering Woods | Ancient, verdant. Trees that have stood longer than memory. The forest breathes. |
| Sunscorched Dunes | Vast and patient. The desert buries everything eventually, and reveals it again. |
| Frostbound Peaks | Silent and sharp. Ice preserves what fire destroys. |
| Ashfall Caldera | Restless. The volcano does not sleep — it waits. |
| Murkmire Swamp | Hidden. What lives here does not want to be found. |
| Crystal Abyss | Luminous. The crystals remember light from before the world learned shadow. |
| Skyfall Ruins | Vertiginous. These towers once reached the heavens. Now they reach for each other. |
| Abyssal Trench | Alien. The deep ocean has its own rules, its own time, its own dark. |

## The Bosses

Each boss is the living embodiment of its island. They are not evil — they are the island's will made flesh, testing whether the Builder is worthy.

| Boss | Island | What They Guard |
|------|--------|----------------|
| Ancient Treant | Whispering Woods | The page of motion — the Dash |
| Pharaoh Construct | Sunscorched Dunes | The page of force — Ground Pound |
| Frost Wyrm | Frostbound Peaks | The page of bridges — Ice Path |
| Magma King | Ashfall Caldera | The page of fire — Fire Lance |
| Mire Hag | Murkmire Swamp | The page of walls — Block Shield |
| Crystal Hydra | Crystal Abyss | The page of shadows — Shadow Step |
| Storm Titan | Skyfall Ruins | The page of winds — Wind Jump |
| Abyssal Leviathan | Abyssal Trench | The page of stone — Stone Skin |

## Tone

The world is wondrous but not whimsical. Stakes are real. Death matters (run gear is lost). But the Tome accumulates — each page earned is permanent, each island conquered is a memory the Tome keeps.

The aesthetic is warm parchment and ink. Discovery is celebrated. The writing on Tome pages uses an archaic, earnest voice — as if written by someone who believed deeply in what they were recording.

## Victory Condition

All eight pages. The Tome is complete. What happens when a Builder finishes Bok? The game doesn't say. That's the point.
